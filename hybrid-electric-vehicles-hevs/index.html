Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs)

Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs) combine an Internal Combustion Engine (ICE) or other propulsion source with batteries, regenerative braking, and an electric motor. This culminates in improved fuel economy.

HEVs rely on a petroleum-based (or an alternative) fuel for power and are not plugged in to charge. HEV batteries are charged by the ICE or other propulsion source and during regenerative braking.
