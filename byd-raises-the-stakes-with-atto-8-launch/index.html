BYD raises the stakes with ATTO 8 launch

Tristan Wiggill April 18, 2026 New Models

Chinese new-energy vehicle giant brings seven-seat plug-in hybrid to market at prices starting from just over R1m

BYD has officially launched the ATTO 8 in South Africa, bringing a large seven-seat plug-in hybrid SUV to a market that is showing growing appetite for electrified family transport.

The ATTO 8, which is sold under the Tang L and Sealion 8 nameplates in other markets, arrives in two variants. The Premium edition is priced at R1 059 900, while the Performance AWD edition carries a sticker price of R1 259 900. 

Two powertrains, one platform

At the heart of both variants is BYD's fifth-generation DM Super Hybrid Technology, which pairs a 1.5-litre turbocharged four-cylinder petrol engine producing 110 kW with one or two electric motors, depending on the specification chosen. 

The Premium model uses a single front-mounted electric motor for a combined system output of 205 kW, drawing on a 19 kWh lithium-iron-phosphate Blade Battery for a claimed electric-only range of 75 km and a total combined range of 892 km.

The Performance AWD variant adds a second electric motor on the rear axle, lifting combined output to 359 kW and 675 Nm. Its larger 35.6 kWh battery delivers a claimed 130 km of pure electric driving, with a combined range of 844 km. The sprint from standstill to 100 km/h takes a claimed 4.9 seconds in this configuration.

Measuring 5 040 mm in length with a 2 950 mm wheelbase, the ATTO 8 is a full-size crossover that positions itself alongside established three-row rivals such as the Audi Q7 and Volvo EX90 in terms of sheer footprint.

Space, specification and a serious warranty

Standard equipment on the Premium model includes a 15.6-inch rotating infotainment display, a 10.25-inch digital instrument cluster, 20-inch alloy wheels, a 10-speaker audio system, wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, and powered, heated and ventilated front seats. 

The Performance model upgrades to 21-inch wheels, a head-up display, adaptive suspension, a 21-speaker sound system, and heated and ventilated second-row seats with massage functionality. Bamboo-wood trim, leather upholstery and a 360-degree camera system feature across the range.

Boot capacity with all three rows in use stands at 270 litres, expanding to 960 litres with the third row folded and 1 960 litres with both rear rows stowed.

Steve Chang, managing director of BYD Auto South Africa, said the launch represented a deepening of the brand's commitment to the local market. "The ATTO 8 combines innovation, space and performance in a way that feels both premium and accessible, whether you are navigating the city or travelling with family over long distances," he said.

Commercial director Mark Handley added that the model was intended to broaden BYD's customer base beyond early adopters. "For us, the ATTO 8 is more than just a new model. It is an opportunity to grow the BYD customer base in South Africa, to reach new households and continue building confidence in our brand," he said.

Each ATTO 8 comes with a five-year/100 000 km vehicle warranty, an eight-year/200 000 km battery warranty, and a five-year/100 000 km maintenance plan.

The launch follows a period of rapid expansion for BYD in South Africa, where the brand has steadily widened its model range from entry-level offerings such as the Dolphin Surf to vehicles that compete squarely in the upper-mid segment. 

The ATTO 8 represents the most direct challenge yet to established European and Japanese three-row SUVs in the country's premium family vehicle space.
