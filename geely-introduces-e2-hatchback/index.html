Geely introduces E2 hatchback

Tristan Wiggill April 14, 2026 New Models

Geely Auto South Africa has introduced the E2, a compact all-electric five-door hatchback aimed at making electric mobility more attainable for everyday urban drivers. The model arrives in two specification grades, Aspire and Apex, priced at R339 900 and R389 900 respectively.

The E2 is built on a dedicated electric vehicle platform that enables a flat cabin floor, improved weight distribution and a long wheelbase relative to its overall footprint. At 4 135 mm in length with a 2 650 mm wheelbase, the car is designed to balance compact exterior dimensions with a practical interior.

Power comes from an 85 kW rear-wheel-drive electric motor producing 150 Nm of torque, paired with a 39.4 kWh lithium iron phosphate battery. Geely claims a WLTP range of up to 325 km and an energy consumption figure of 15.2 kWh per 100 km. The sprint from 0 to 100 km/h takes 11.5 seconds, with a top speed of 130 km/h.

Charging options include AC top-up from 10 to 100% in approximately 6.5 hours, and DC fast charging from 30 to 80% in around 25 minutes at up to 70 kW. The E2 also supports Vehicle-to-Load (V2L) functionality, allowing it to power external devices, and includes a battery pre-heating feature.

Inside, both variants feature a dual-display setup comprising a 14.6-inch infotainment touchscreen and an 8.8-inch digital instrument cluster. The boot offers 375 litres of space, expandable to 1 320 litres, with an additional 70-litre front trunk. More than 30 storage compartments are distributed throughout the cabin.

The Aspire grade covers the fundamentals with fabric seats, manual air conditioning, a rear camera, keyless entry and a four-speaker audio system. The Apex adds vegan leather upholstery, a power-adjustable driver's seat, wireless charging, ambient lighting, a six-speaker audio system and a 360-degree panoramic camera with transparent chassis view.

On the safety front, all models include ABS, ESC, six airbags, traction control, hill-start assist and tyre pressure monitoring. The Apex extends this with a full suite of advanced driver assistance systems, including adaptive cruise control, automatic emergency braking, lane departure warning and forward collision warning.

Connectivity across the range is handled by Geely's Flyme Auto Smart System, with Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, Bluetooth and 4G as standard.

Ownership benefits include a four-year or 150 000 km vehicle warranty, an eight-year or 200 000 km battery warranty, a three-year or 200 000 km service plan, and five-year unlimited kilometre roadside assistance. A wallbox home charger is included, subject to terms and conditions via Geely Finance.

Geely currently operates 32 dealerships in South Africa and has indicated plans to grow that network to 40 by the end of 2026.
