About

South Africa is in the middle of a significant shift in how people and goods move. The government has set out a formal roadmap to transition the country's automotive industry by 2035. 

Export markets in Europe are already closing their doors to petrol and diesel vehicles. And a new generation of affordable electric models is arriving on our shores. 

Things are moving quickly. It can be hard to know what is happening and what it means for you. That is what EV News is here for.

We are a South African platform dedicated to covering the electric and New Energy Vehicle (NEV) industry honestly and clearly.  We track what is happening across the full spectrum of EVs, from passenger cars and motorbikes to buses, trucks, and vans. 

We cover news, policy developments, new model arrivals, charging infrastructure, battery technology, and the real-world experience of owning and running an electric vehicle in this country.

Why this matters here, specifically

South Africa's EV story is not the same as Europe's or China's. We have our own grid challenges, our own infrastructure gaps, our own import duties, and our own opportunities. 

Private companies are already building off-grid solar-powered charging networks along national roads. Affordable models are beginning to bring EVs within reach of a broader market. 

The question of whether South Africa can manufacture EVs locally, and what that means for jobs and the economy, is very much alive. These are the conversations we follow.

Who we write for

EV News is for anyone trying to make sense of this transition. 

That includes people thinking about buying their first EV, businesses considering fleet electrification, professionals working in the automotive and energy sectors, and anyone who wants to understand where South Africa's transport future is heading.

We do not assume you already know the industry. We write in plain language, explain what things mean, and focus on what is relevant to life and work in South Africa.

What we cover

We publish news, analysis, policy updates, infrastructure developments, model reviews, and case studies. We keep an eye on what is happening globally when it shapes what is coming here, but our focus is always local.
