Dongfeng Nammi 06 EV Arrives in SA

Tristan Wiggill April 12, 2026 New Models

 

Dongfeng has introduced its latest electric vehicle to the South African market with the arrival of the Nammi 06, a compact crossover SUV that undercuts rivals by a significant margin.

Priced from R499,900, the Nammi 06 is now the country’s most affordable fully electric SUV, undercutting previous entry-level models such as the BYD Atto 3 and Geely E5 by approximately R200,000.

This marks the third model from the state-owned Chinese manufacturer to reach local shores following the Box hatchback and 007 sedan, which launched in late 2025.

Distributed by E Auto Motor, the Nammi 06 is being dispatched to dealerships in Boksburg, Pretoria, Cape Town, and Durban, where test drives are already available.

The move reflects the accelerating influx of affordable Chinese EVs into South Africa, driven by growing demand for sustainable transport amid rising fuel costs and environmental concerns.

The Nammi 06 is offered in two variants: the E1 and the range-topping E3. Both are powered by a single front-mounted permanent magnet synchronous electric motor producing 120kW and 230Nm of torque.

This setup enables a 0-100km/h sprint in 7.8 seconds and a top speed of 150km/h. The entry-level E1 features a 44.94kWh lithium iron phosphate (LFP) battery, delivering a claimed 401km range under the Chinese Light-Duty Vehicle Test Cycle (CLTC).

Real-world expectations under the more stringent Worldwide Harmonised Light Vehicle Test Procedure (WLTP) are closer to 300km. DC fast charging from 30 to 80 percent takes around 30 minutes.

The E3 upgrades to a larger 51.87kWh battery for a CLTC range of 471km (approximately 350km WLTP), with faster DC charging capability of up to 167kW, achieving 30-80 percent in 18 minutes.

Both models support vehicle-to-load (V2L) functionality, making them practical for outdoor use or power outages. Styling follows Dongfeng’s modern EV design language, with sleek lines, LED lighting, roof rails, and a practical 500-litre boot accessible via a split-opening tailgate.

The E1 rides on 17-inch alloy wheels, while the E3 gains 18-inch rims, a panoramic sunroof, and heated mirrors.

Inside, both offer leatherette upholstery, a multifunction steering wheel, dual digital displays (8.8-inch driver’s instrument cluster and 12.8-inch touchscreen infotainment), wireless smartphone charging, and a six-speaker audio system.

Higher trims add ambient lighting, ventilated and heated driver’s seat with memory function, and advanced driver assistance systems including adaptive cruise control, 360-degree camera, lane-keep assist, and automatic emergency braking.

Safety equipment is comprehensive across the range, with six airbags, electronic stability control, hill-start assist, tyre-pressure monitoring, and multiple active safety features.

All models come with a five-year/100,000km vehicle warranty, five-year/100,000km service plan, and eight-year/200,000km battery warranty.

The Nammi 06’s competitive pricing and feature set position it strongly against established players in South Africa’s burgeoning EV segment.

As infrastructure improves and government incentives evolve, models like this could accelerate the transition to electric mobility for everyday South African motorists.

Industry observers will watch closely to see whether this latest Dongfeng offering can capture meaningful market share in a field increasingly crowded with Chinese brands.
